Jianing(Aurora) Qian

I am a final-year Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the University of Pennsylvania, advised by Prof. Dinesh Jayaraman in the GRASP Lab. In 2023, I received the AWS Fellowship for research on trustworthy AI.

My research lies at the intersection of robot learning, computer vision, and machine learning. I investigate how structured, hierarchical representations—particularly those derived from foundation models—can serve as the basis for world models that enable long-horizon planning and generalization. My goal is to develop scalable learning and decision-making systems for embodied agents.

Prior to my Ph.D., I received my B.S. and M.S. in Computer Science and Robotics from Carnegie Mellon University, where I worked with Prof. David Held and Prof. Howie Choset. In 2024, I was a research intern at the Robotics and AI Institute (RAI), where I worked on hierarchical control policies for joint base-arm coordination in mobile manipulation.
